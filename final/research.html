The Impact of the Manitowoc Shipyard During World War II: The Relationship Between National Pride and Production During World War II

January 2018 - Present

This paper addresses the societal impact of small town America during the Second World War, specifically of Manitowoc, Wisconsin during World War II. Socially the city of Manitowoc was impacted by the abrupt increase in population from 1941-1945. In September of 1940 Manitowoc Shipbuilding Co. was given a contract to construct 10 Submarines for the war effort. The number of workers who poured into the shipyard to work was substantial. During World War II The Manitowoc Shipbuilding Co. employed 7,000 people working around the clock for the war years. Those employed at the shipyard included men, a large number amount of women and youth workers, either still being educated in local high schools or recently graduated. Manitowoc grew substantially as workers and crewmembers were being bused in from neighboring communities and contributing to the growth of the local economy. Manitowoc became a boomtown during the war, the vibrant lifestyle which took place between the parties, and dances and the local establishments which transformed Manitowoc into what was common of war-time cities. A community which came together for the good of the country rather than self-recognition and this idea of working for the country and working for “Uncle Sam” was a prime morale boost for those who worked in the Shipyard and lived in Manitowoc, Wisconsin.






The 17th Amendment Did Not Hinder Party Machines But Caused a Federal Crisis

January 2018 - Present

The Progressive Era in the United States was a time profound reform, with one of the main objectives of progressive politicians being to eliminate government corruption. The progressive ideas, as well as populist ideas of having the people have more input rather than party dominance and big money politics, was at the center of the progressive drive. Rather than the norm of what government can do for society. This emerging philosophy instead centered on the question of, what can society do for government and how can the people, rather than political machines, or money? Implementation of this change, which would help society collectively, a constitutional change would be necessary. On April 8th, 1913, the Seventeenth Amendment was ratified, Connecticut was the last state needed to secure the three-quarters needed to pass the amendment. The amendment’s main purpose was to curtail the power of the party bosses who have been building up influence and securing power in state legislatures which prior to the amendment chose the senators. Although the amendment was used as a tool to promote democracy, senatorial candidates had to campaign like never before, because of the amendment’s main goal of limiting agendas - which were set by party bosses - and regulating the nomination process.
